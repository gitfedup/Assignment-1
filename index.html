Project Idea

Virtual 3D Trade Show with Hologram



Overview

My project is to create an online B2B platform with external hardware hologram function so buyers can have a better visualisation of the potential products they are purchasing. The platform will be a website that has a register function for buyers and a subscription function for brands.

The buyer will have an option that takes them on a virtual walk through of the aisles in the ‘trade show’ where brands will showcase the products they’re selling for that season. The buyer can choose to interact with sales staff that are available for live voice chat and the buyer can view products by entering the brands stand. The buyer can then look at products more closely by clicking on a product which will result in their local 3D hologram being displayed on their desk in front of them. Ideally the product will display in 1:1 size ratio and will be in a slow spinning display with full colour.

Motivation

Reed Gift Fairs have an exhibitor profile of 2,694 brands over 82 different gift, home & lifestyle categories. Their database is reaches out to 42,000+ buyers globally. Their physical trade shows are extremely costly to the brands that exhibit and to the buyers that must travel to them.

My platform will assist buyers to do their job without the cost and time that goes into attending a physical trade show. Aside from the cost and time factors, recent global health issues also play a major part in move towards a virtual event over a physical one. Costs will still be involved however, I imagine they will be a fraction of the cost of a physical show and convenience is also a major part of the appeal.

Description

My platform and hardware extension will have specific elements that are an integral part of why this software is successful.

Being ultimately user-friendly this platform will be very easy to use so that buyers with all levels of technical experience can enjoy and work effectively without wasting time trying to figure it out. After the initial login the buyer is simply clicking arrows to point in a direction to navigate their way to what would look like the aisles of a physical trade show, like how google maps works when using the street view function. Once the buyer comes across a brand or product they are considering, they click on the brand icon and can choose to interact with the representative or simply browse the product in the trade show ‘shop’. The brand representative would be able to communicate via VOIP or text chat box to ask/receive questions or take request for a ‘showing’ appointment.

At physical trade shows, buyers can behave incognito so to avoid unwelcome attention from brands. This often results in missed opportunities on the brands behalf which, as a business who are paying for a service to benefit the buyer, is not ideal. Therefore, a function is built into the program so that when a pre-flagged buyer comes within close virtual proximity to your ‘shop’, you get a warning. The live function from the representative’s end always shows all the customers within their shop and also provides updates from time spent away.

A live look at the brands shop back end, would show all buyers within their current space by way of a map. Think of Harry Potter’s Marauder's Map but being able to virtually tap on a customer’s shoulder and introduce yourself and start a conversation.

In order to attend the trade show, buyers must register with their business and trading name, business type and ABN. All buyers are verified before being approved and a minimum purchase agreement must be adhered to unless an exemption has been prior approved. This is to remove trade show tourists, to protect the IP of the brands and keep the event a true trade only event. Putting these functions and requirements into place will make sure that the representatives are only spending time with genuine buyers and not ‘time-wasters’.

When buyers are in the shop, items that are clicked on will pop up on the buyers external hardware device in hologram form with ratio index. From there, the customer can choose to add it to their shopping cart. This hologram function is particularly unique and gives more well rounded view of the item they are considering.

To streamline the process for buyers, leaving the check out process until they have finished all purchasing from each brand will be a big time-saver and will also help to keep track of their spending.

Tools & Technologies

Adobe Flash or Javascript is required for the shop aisle viewing portion of the platform. CAD will also be required to design the shop fronts. A 3D photographer and their equipment needs to be used by the brands and a hologram projector needs to be sourced by the buyer or provided to the buyer. For adding to cart and shopping function, Shopify would work with some adjustments. Teams can be used for the rep to buyer communication. A tracking software would also be required for the function where the rep can view which buyers are within their store.

Skills Required

A web developer is required for this project. A visual merchandiser and web designer also. Hardware required is a personal hologram device and software to bring at all together.

Drawbacks or Obstacles

A drawback is that this virtual trade show concept already exists but without the hologram function. I have not experienced the tools, functions and display offered by these businesses because you need industry access to view the platform. Therefore, I am unaware of the direct competition at this point and need further research for the product to determine it specifically unique and viable.

The personal hologram device is a product that needs work over the next 5 years to improve the technology and make it more commercially available.

Outcome

If this project is successful, many trade events across multiple industries that would normally require physical attendance would become obsolete. Therefore, allowing businesses to spend their time and money within other areas of the business rather than needing to use it for exorbitant traveling costs. An added bonus is the reduction in greenhouse gasses produced by the transport network because buyers will no longer need to use the network and the issue of increased risk to health due to the spread of virus is eliminated.
